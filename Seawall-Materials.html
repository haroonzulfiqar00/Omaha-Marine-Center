Why are so many people choosing vinyl sheet piling?

Vinyl sheet piling is quickly becoming the most popular product to use for construction of bulkheads and seawalls.

Materials like wood and concrete have been widely used in the past, but the use of these traditional materials is becoming virtually obsolete. With the advent of vinyl sheet piling, homeowners with oceanfront or lakefront property can now rely on the newest manufacturing technology to receive the best product on the market today.

Cost Efficiency

Vinyl seawalls offer lasting value. While the initial cost may be comparable to traditional materials, vinyl sheet piling lasts much longer, reducing future repair and replacement expenses. It also adds value to your property, giving buyers confidence that the seawall will stand the test of time. Inferior materials simply can't match the durability of our modern solutions.

Consistent Appearance

Wood fades and requires frequent repainting to maintain its appearance, while vinyl retains its color and looks great for years with minimal upkeep.

UV Resistance

Most materials break down over time from exposure to the sun's UV rays, but vinyl sheet piling is built to resist this damage, keeping its strength and appearance far longer.

Unaffected by Marine Borers

Marine borers can quickly damage wooden seawalls by drilling into them, weakening their structure over time. Vinyl, however, offers no food source or attraction for these pests, keeping your seawall strong and intact for years.

Easy Installation

Vinyl sheet piling installs quickly and easily compared to wood, which can warp or have imperfections that slow the process. Because vinyl is precisely manufactured, each panel is consistent - ensuring a smooth installation and minimal disruption to your property.

Better Warranty

Vinyl sheet piling comes with a 50-year manufacturer's warranty, reflecting its long-lasting durability. Installed by a certified dealer, your seawall is fully protected, making vinyl the superior choice over wood, aluminum, steel, or concrete.
